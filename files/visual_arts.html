Visual Arts

At Washington University Nursery School we offer children many opportunities to express themselves creatively and artistically.

We value visual arts, music, and dramatic play as important building blocks in constructing learning. In the visual arts, the emphasis is on process and exploration of materials, not only the final product.

These art experiences can increase attention span, improve fine motor skills, strengthen confidence and emotional well-being, and build independence.

Materials and experiences include:

  • Drawing with crayons, pencils, chalk, markers, and sidewalk chalk.
  • Painting with fingers, brushes, feathers, sponges, leaves, vegetables, and other materials.
  • Printmaking with rubber stamps, potato shapes, and various objects.
  • Working with clay and doughs used for shaping, building, and modeling.
  • Cutting, tearing, gluing, and stapling.
  • Shaping/forming and stringing beads.
  • Creating collages from fabrics, paper, pictures, and three-dimensional items.
  • Building three-dimensional constructions using boxes, tubes, cartons, and other recyclable materials.
  • Creating pictures or structures using wire, clay, wood, and other materials.
