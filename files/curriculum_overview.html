Curriculum Overview

Our curriculum is play-based and developmentally appropriate. Children build social-emotional skills, language, math thinking, motor coordination, and creative expression through hands-on classroom and outdoor experiences.





Academic Development

Language, math, science, and social studies concepts are developed through conversation, storytelling, counting, patterning, experimentation, and community-focused inquiry.

Read Academic Development (language, math, science, social studies)





Social & Emotional Development

Children strengthen self-esteem, collaboration, self-expression, and problem-solving through daily routines, group projects, and guided conflict resolution.

Read Social-Emotional Learning





Language & Literacy

Language grows through conversation, storytelling, songs, dramatic play, early word recognition, and children dictating and writing their own stories.

Read Language & Literacy





Gross & Fine Motor Skills

Children practice coordination and control through climbing, riding, playground games, music and movement, drawing, writing, cutting, and manipulative materials.

Read Gross & Fine Motor Skills
