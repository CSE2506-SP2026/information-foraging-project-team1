Curriculum Overview

At Washington University Nursery School, we believe children are unique individuals with their own patterns and timetables for social, emotional, intellectual, and physical development. We support and encourage this growth through a child-centered, process-oriented, play-based curriculum built on Constructivist theories and practices, guided by educational theorists including Piaget, Dewey, Vygotsky, DeVries, and others.

  • Classroom experiences are structured around ideas and topics relevant to students, with knowledge built through language, discussion, experimentation, and meaningful materials.
  • Hands-on learning uses a variety of materials, engages multiple senses, and encourages exploration and investigation.
  • Each child is allowed to interact with the environment at his/her own pace, individually, with a partner, or in larger groups.
  • Teachers ask open-ended questions, allow time for thoughtful responses, listen respectfully, and gently guide children as reasoning skills develop.
  • Students' ideas are respected, independent thinking is encouraged, and children are empowered to become problem solvers.
  • Our diverse, multi-cultural community is used to highlight similarities and differences among individuals, families, and cultures.

Washington University Nursery School's open curriculum framework is developmentally appropriate and based on the philosophy that children learn most effectively through play. Using a learning center approach, each teaching team offers experiences in art, writing, science, dramatic play, blocks, manipulatives, and literacy while planning for each child's social, emotional, intellectual, and physical developmental needs.
